# COCD
Chinese Olfactory Corpus

A large-scale Chinese olfactory sentence dataset with entity annotation for computational olfactory perception research.

Overview

Language plays a pivotal role in artificial olfactory perception, serving as a crucial bridge that translates chemical stimuli into human experience. The mapping from olfactory stimuli to linguistic descriptions constitutes the foundational basis for modeling artificial olfaction with human-like descriptive capabilities. However, existing publicly available olfactory corpora are constrained by cultural bias and limited linguistic diversity, with most datasets predominantly reflecting Western odor profiles.

To address this gap, we constructed the Chinese Olfactory Corpus — a large-scale, entity-annotated corpus of olfactory sentences in Chinese — using a dual iterative strategy assisted by large language models (LLMs). The dataset enables training and evaluation of semantic language models that achieve a more nuanced and perceptually grounded mapping of Chinese olfactory experience.
